Hi team,

We have a retention problem. People log for a few days, then forget about the app. One user told me: "I logged for 2 weeks straight and then realized I never actually *looked back* at any of it."

I want to add a **Weekly Reflection** feature:

- Every Sunday evening (or whenever the user configures), auto-generate a summary of their week
- Best day / worst day (by mood or energy)
- Most common tags that week
- Trend direction — is mood generally improving, declining, or flat?
- A reflection prompt: "What would you do differently next week?"

Bonus: let users optionally **share their weekly digest** with a friend, mentor, or accountability partner. Not the raw data — just the summary with highlights.

Questions I want us to think about:

- What makes a good summary vs. just restating the data? "Your average mood was 3.4" is boring. "You had a rough start to the week but bounced back strong by Thursday" is interesting.
- What tone should the summary have? Clinical? Warm and encouraging? Neutral?
- How do we handle a terrible week sensitively? We can't just say "This was your worst week in a month!" without care.
- If someone shares their digest — do they pick what to include? Or is it a fixed format?
- What if someone only logged 1-2 times that week? Is the digest still useful?

This could be the hook that keeps people coming back.
